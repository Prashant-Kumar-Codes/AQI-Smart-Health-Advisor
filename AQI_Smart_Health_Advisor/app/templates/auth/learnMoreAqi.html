Why AQI Ranges Matter

Understanding AQI ranges helps you:

  • Decide whether it is safe to go outside
  • Protect vulnerable family members
  • Plan work, exercise, and travel safely
  • Reduce long-term health risks caused by air pollution

Knowing how to read AQI values enables timely action and better health decisions.

‚ù§Ô∏è Health Effects of Poor Air Quality

Breathing polluted air can affect both short-term well-being and long-term health. Even people who feel healthy can experience problems when air quality remains poor for extended periods. Some groups, such as children, elderly people, and individuals with existing health conditions, are at higher risk.

Short-Term Health Effects

Poor air quality can cause immediate symptoms, including:

  • Irritation in the eyes, nose, and throat
  • Coughing and shortness of breath
  • Headaches and dizziness
  • Fatigue and reduced concentration
  • Worsening of asthma symptoms

These effects may appear within hours of exposure, especially during high AQI levels.

Long-Term Health Effects

Continuous exposure to polluted air can lead to serious health conditions, such as:

  • Chronic respiratory diseases (asthma, bronchitis, COPD)
  • Reduced lung function and lung damage
  • Increased risk of heart disease and stroke
  • High blood pressure and cardiovascular stress
  • Weakened immune system

Long-term exposure increases the risk even if symptoms are not immediately visible.

Why Awareness Matters

Understanding the health effects of polluted air helps people take timely preventive steps. Reducing exposure, following safety guidelines, and monitoring AQI regularly can significantly lower health risks and improve quality of life.
